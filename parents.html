Your child can become the one who protects your entire family online

COMPUDON Junior doesn't just keep children safe — it builds the judgement, confidence and capability to lead their digital lives. For ages 8–14.

What your child walks away with

Capability, not just caution

They spot the trick

Fake profiles, phishing links, manipulative design — your child learns to recognise them before reacting.

They think before they share

Media literacy and critical thinking — the habit of pausing, questioning, and verifying before passing anything on.

They understand AI

How AI works, why it sometimes gets things wrong, and how to use it responsibly — not just consume it passively.

They protect the family

Children who complete the championship often become the household's first line of digital defence — teaching parents what they've learned.

Why this matters now

The context behind the capability

These are the reasons the skills above are no longer optional.

2 million jobs at risk

NITI Aayog warned AI-driven automation could displace up to two million jobs in India's tech-services sector by 2031.

5× more safety incidents

NCRB data shows a five-fold rise in internet-facilitated child-safety incidents in India between 2021 and 2023.

#1 in misinformation risk

The World Economic Forum ranked India first for misinformation and disinformation risk — two years running.

NEP now grades this

India's NEP mandates report cards reflecting both academic and non-academic achievement — creativity and critical thinking count.

Sources: NITI Aayog / NASSCOM / BCG (Oct 2025) · NCRB Crime in India 2023 · WEF Global Risks Report 2024–25 · NEP 2024–25, Ministry of Education.
