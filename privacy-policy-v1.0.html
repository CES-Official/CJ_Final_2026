Introduction

CyberLearning Educational Society (Regd.) ("CES", "we", "our", or "us") is committed to protecting the privacy and personal data of every participant, parent, guardian, educator, school, volunteer, and visitor who interacts with COMPUDON Junior.

COMPUDON Junior is an educational initiative developed and operated by CyberLearning Educational Society (Regd.) to promote digital citizenship, cyber safety, media literacy, critical thinking, responsible technology use, and related educational competencies among school students.

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, share, protect, retain, and otherwise process Personal Data in connection with the COMPUDON Junior platform, championship, educational programs, assessments, certifications, events, communications, websites, mobile applications, and associated services (collectively referred to as the "Services").

We recognise that children deserve the highest standards of privacy protection. Accordingly, we are committed to processing children's Personal Data responsibly, transparently, fairly, and only for legitimate educational purposes in accordance with applicable law.

This Privacy Policy has been prepared primarily in accordance with the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 ("DPDP Act"), together with applicable rules, regulations, governmental notifications, and other applicable laws in force in India. Where appropriate, this Policy also reflects internationally recognised privacy principles commonly adopted by educational institutions and child-focused digital platforms.

5

Legal Basis for Processing

CyberLearning Educational Society (Regd.) processes Personal Data only where there is a lawful basis for doing so under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023, and other applicable laws.

A. Verifiable Consent

Where required by applicable law, including for the processing of a Child's Personal Data, we process Personal Data only after obtaining valid, informed, specific, unconditional, and verifiable consent from the Parent or lawful guardian. Parents or lawful guardians may withdraw their consent at any time in accordance with this Privacy Policy. Withdrawal of consent shall not affect the lawfulness of processing undertaken prior to such withdrawal.

B. Legitimate Educational Activities

Personal Data is processed only to the extent reasonably necessary for delivering educational programs, conducting assessments, evaluating performance, issuing certificates, communicating with participants and schools, and administering the COMPUDON Junior Services.

C. Compliance with Legal Obligations

We may process Personal Data where necessary to comply with applicable legal obligations, governmental directions, judicial orders, regulatory requirements, accounting obligations, audit requirements, or law enforcement requests, to the extent permitted under applicable law.

D. Protection of Rights and Security

Personal Data may also be processed where reasonably necessary to protect the security and integrity of our Services; prevent fraud, misuse, unauthorised access, or unlawful activities; investigate complaints or security incidents; establish, exercise, or defend legal claims; or protect the rights, safety, or property of participants, schools, CyberLearning Educational Society (Regd.), or other stakeholders.

6

Children's Personal Data and Verifiable Parental Consent

COMPUDON Junior is designed primarily for school students. We recognise that children require enhanced privacy protections, and we are committed to processing their Personal Data responsibly and in accordance with applicable law.

Before collecting or processing the Personal Data of a Child, we obtain verifiable consent from the Parent or lawful guardian. Such verification may include one or more of the following methods:

  • Verification through the participating school
  • One-Time Password (OTP) verification
  • Verification using official contact details provided by the Parent or school
  • Digitally authenticated consent mechanisms
  • Any other reasonable verification method permitted under applicable law
We do not knowingly:
  • Undertake behavioural monitoring of children
  • Profile children for commercial purposes
  • Serve targeted advertising to children
  • Sell or commercially exploit children's Personal Data
  • Process children's Personal Data in any manner prohibited under applicable law

If we become aware that Personal Data has been collected without the required parental authorisation, we will take reasonable steps to verify the circumstances and, where appropriate, delete such Personal Data in accordance with applicable law.

7

Sharing of Personal Data

CyberLearning Educational Society (Regd.) respects the confidentiality of Personal Data and does not sell, rent, license, trade, or otherwise commercially exploit Personal Data collected through the COMPUDON Junior Services.

Personal Data may be shared only where reasonably necessary for the purposes described in this Privacy Policy and only with appropriate safeguards.

A. Participating Schools

We may share relevant information relating to participating students with their respective schools for legitimate educational purposes, including registration verification, coordination of championship activities, performance reports, certificates and awards, and academic administration.

B. Authorised Service Providers

We may engage carefully selected third-party service providers to perform services on our behalf, including cloud hosting, email and communication services, learning management platforms, certificate generation, payment processing, technical support, cybersecurity services, and other operational support services. Such service providers process Personal Data only under written contractual obligations requiring appropriate confidentiality, security, and data protection measures. (See Annexure C for categories of service providers.)

C. Legal and Regulatory Authorities

We may disclose Personal Data where disclosure is required under applicable law, pursuant to a lawful governmental request, in compliance with a judicial order, to comply with regulatory requirements, to establish, exercise, or defend legal claims, or to protect the rights, safety, property, or security of participants, schools, or CyberLearning Educational Society (Regd.).

D. Business Reorganisation

If CyberLearning Educational Society (Regd.) undergoes a lawful merger, amalgamation, demerger, reorganisation, or similar organisational change, Personal Data may be transferred to the successor entity, subject to applicable legal requirements and appropriate safeguards.

8

International Transfer of Personal Data

CyberLearning Educational Society (Regd.) primarily processes Personal Data within India. However, certain technology infrastructure, cloud services, software platforms, communication systems, or other operational services used in connection with the COMPUDON Junior Services may involve the processing or storage of Personal Data outside the territory of India where permitted under applicable law.

Where Personal Data is transferred outside India, we will take reasonable steps to ensure that such transfers are carried out in accordance with applicable legal requirements and that appropriate contractual, technical, and organisational safeguards are implemented to protect Personal Data.

We do not intentionally transfer Personal Data to jurisdictions that are prohibited or restricted under applicable Indian law.

9

Security of Personal Data

Protecting children's Personal Data is a fundamental responsibility of CyberLearning Educational Society (Regd.). We implement reasonable administrative, technical, physical, and organisational measures designed to safeguard Personal Data against unauthorised access, disclosure, alteration, destruction, accidental loss, misuse, or other unauthorised processing.

Our information security measures may include, where appropriate:

  • Access controls based on business need
  • Authentication mechanisms for authorised users
  • Encryption of Personal Data during transmission and, where appropriate, while stored
  • Secure hosting environments
  • Periodic security reviews
  • Monitoring of systems for unauthorised activities
  • Backup and recovery procedures
  • Staff awareness and confidentiality obligations
  • Incident response procedures

Although we strive to use commercially reasonable measures to protect Personal Data, no method of electronic transmission, storage, or processing can be guaranteed to be completely secure. Users are also responsible for protecting their own login credentials, devices, and account information where applicable.

10

Data Retention and Deletion

CyberLearning Educational Society (Regd.) retains Personal Data only for as long as reasonably necessary to fulfil the purposes described in this Privacy Policy, comply with applicable legal obligations, resolve disputes, enforce legal rights, maintain academic records, or satisfy legitimate administrative requirements.

The applicable retention periods for different categories of Personal Data are described in Annexure B — Data Retention Schedule, which forms an integral part of this Privacy Policy.

At the end of the applicable retention period, Personal Data will be securely deleted, permanently anonymised, or retained only where continued retention is required or permitted under applicable law.

Where a Parent or Data Principal exercises a valid right to erasure, we will delete the relevant Personal Data unless continued retention is required or permitted under applicable law.

11

Rights of Data Principals

Subject to applicable law, Data Principals may exercise the rights available under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023, including:

  • Obtain information regarding the Personal Data being processed by us
  • Request correction, completion, or updating of inaccurate or incomplete Personal Data
  • Request erasure of Personal Data where such erasure is required or permitted under applicable law
  • Withdraw previously provided consent, where processing is based on consent
  • Seek redressal of grievances relating to the processing of Personal Data
  • Nominate another individual to exercise rights on their behalf, where permitted under applicable law

The exercise of these rights may be subject to reasonable verification of identity and any limitations, exceptions, or obligations prescribed under applicable law. Requests relating to Personal Data may be submitted to the Grievance Officer. We will acknowledge and respond to such requests within the timelines prescribed under applicable law or, where no statutory timeline exists, within a reasonable period.

The procedure for exercising these rights is set out in Annexure D.

12

Withdrawal of Consent

Where Personal Data is processed based on consent, the Parent or lawful guardian may withdraw such consent at any time. Withdrawal of consent shall not affect the lawfulness of any processing undertaken before such withdrawal.

Upon receiving a valid request for withdrawal of consent, we will:

  • Discontinue further processing of Personal Data to the extent required by applicable law
  • Discontinue the Child's participation in the Services where continued processing is necessary for participation
  • Securely delete or anonymise the relevant Personal Data in accordance with this Privacy Policy and our Data Retention Schedule, unless continued retention is required or permitted under applicable law
  • Notify the Parent where any requested action cannot be fully implemented due to applicable legal, regulatory, academic, or contractual obligations

Withdrawal of consent may affect our ability to provide certain Services or educational activities.

13

Personal Data Breach Response

CyberLearning Educational Society (Regd.) maintains procedures designed to identify, assess, manage, and respond to incidents involving Personal Data. If we become aware of a Personal Data breach that requires notification under applicable law, we will take appropriate steps to:

  • Investigate the incident
  • Contain and mitigate the impact of the incident
  • Assess the nature and scope of the breach
  • Notify the appropriate authorities where required under applicable law
  • Notify affected individuals where required under applicable law
  • Implement corrective measures designed to reduce the likelihood of similar incidents occurring in the future

Our internal incident response procedures are periodically reviewed and updated to reflect changes in technology, operational practices, and applicable legal requirements.

14

Grievance Redressal

CyberLearning Educational Society (Regd.) is committed to addressing privacy-related concerns promptly, fairly, and transparently. All privacy-related requests, grievances, and notices should be submitted electronically to the Grievance Officer at privacy@compudonjunior.com.

We encourage individuals to first contact the Grievance Officer so that concerns may be resolved promptly. Where an individual remains dissatisfied after completion of our internal grievance process, they may exercise any remedies available under applicable law, including those before the appropriate statutory authority.

15

Changes to this Privacy Policy

We may amend or update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in applicable law, regulatory guidance, technology, our Services, or our data processing practices. The revised version will be published on the COMPUDON Junior website together with the updated "Last Updated" date.

Where required by applicable law, we will provide appropriate notice of material changes and obtain fresh consent where legally necessary. Continued use of the Services after the effective date of the revised Privacy Policy shall constitute acceptance of the revised Policy to the extent permitted by applicable law.

Annexure D Procedure for Exercising Data Principal Rights
▼

1. Purpose

This Annexure explains the procedure by which Data Principals, Parents, or lawful guardians may exercise their rights under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023, in relation to Personal Data processed by CyberLearning Educational Society (Regd.) for the COMPUDON Junior Services.

2. Who May Submit a Request

Requests under this Annexure may be submitted by:

  • A Data Principal
  • A Parent or lawful guardian acting on behalf of a Child
  • A person lawfully nominated or otherwise authorised to act on behalf of a Data Principal, where permitted under applicable law

3. Rights That May Be Exercised

Subject to applicable law, requests may relate to one or more of the following:

  • Request access to information regarding Personal Data being processed
  • Request correction, completion, or updating of inaccurate or incomplete Personal Data
  • Request erasure of Personal Data where such erasure is required or permitted under applicable law
  • Withdraw previously provided consent
  • Submit a grievance regarding the processing of Personal Data
  • Exercise any other right available under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023

4. How to Submit a Request

Requests should be submitted by email to: privacy@compudonjunior.com

To enable timely processing, the request should include, where applicable:

  • Full name of the requester
  • Relationship with the student (if applicable)
  • Student name and school
  • Contact details
  • Description of the request
  • Any supporting information reasonably necessary to identify the relevant records

We may request additional information where reasonably necessary to verify the identity or authority of the requester before processing the request.
