What CBSE Requires How COMPUDON Junior Delivers It
Circular Skill-81/2025
Digital Citizenship (Code 913) mandatory as Kaushal Bodh subject, Classes VI–VIII, with dedicated timetable periods and internal assessment. 		COMPUDON Junior's 12-module curriculum for Grade 6+ maps directly to Kaushal Bodh's Digital Citizenship competencies. Practice exams and the championship serve as assessment-ready activities your school can use for internal evaluation.
Circular Acad-39/2026
Cyber Clubs — schools must establish Cyber Clubs with structured, regular cyber awareness activities. 		COMPUDON Junior is the structured activity programme for your Cyber Club — six modules (Grades 3–5) or twelve modules (Grade 6+), with stories, exercises, and Think–Pair–Do activities ready to run. No planning required.
Circular Acad-39/2026
Cyber Jagrookta Diwas — structured cyber awareness activities on the first Wednesday of every month. 		Each COMPUDON Junior module is designed as a standalone session. Assign one module per month to your Cyber Jagrookta Diwas calendar — content, exercises, and discussion prompts included.
Circular Acad-39/2026
Cyber Safety Pledge — schools must circulate a pledge among students, signed by parents. 		COMPUDON Junior's registration requires parental consent. A Cyber Safety Pledge template aligned to the CBSE directive is included for all participating schools — ready to print and send home. View the pledge →
NEP 2020 / NCF 2023
Promote 21st-century skills, digital literacy, critical thinking, and competency-based learning. 		COMPUDON Junior covers digital literacy, cyber safety, media literacy, critical thinking, and AI awareness — all six NEP 21st-century skill areas, delivered through competency-based, story-driven pedagogy aligned to NCF 2023.
DPDP Act 2023
India's data protection law — schools must teach students about data privacy and digital rights. 		Module 3 (Protecting Privacy and Digital Identity) teaches data protection principles aligned to DPDP Act concepts, in age-appropriate language students aged 8–14 can understand and apply.
What Your Students Learn

Six essential skills for Grades 3–5. Twelve modules for Grade 6+.

Every module is age-appropriate, story-driven, and built around the Think First. habit — the reflex that makes every skill stick.

MODULE 01

Managing Harmful Interactions

Recognise online harassment, respond with a five-step safety plan, and know when and how to report.

MODULE 02

Resisting Manipulation

See through fake offers, false urgency, and manipulative design — online and in advertising.

MODULE 03

Protecting Privacy & Identity

Understand digital footprints, data protection, and the DPDP Act in age-appropriate language.

MODULE 04

Critical Thinking & Creativity

Question, analyse, and build — not just consume. The durable competency at the core of NCF 2023.

MODULE 05

Media Literacy

Spot misinformation, trace sources, resist confirmation bias, and question what they see online.

MODULE 06

AI Literacy & Responsibilities

Understand AI's limits, verify its answers, and use it responsibly — aligned to CBSE AI (901).

Grade 6+ students cover all six areas across twelve deeper modules, including data fundamentals, AI ethics, and advanced disinformation resistance.
