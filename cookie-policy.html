Purpose

This Cookie Policy explains how CyberLearning Educational Society (Regd.) ("CyberLearning," "we," "our," or "us") uses cookies and similar technologies when you visit the COMPUDON Junior website or use related online services.

This Cookie Policy should be read together with the COMPUDON Junior Privacy Policy, which explains how Personal Data is collected, processed, shared, retained, and protected.

2

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your computer, mobile device, or other internet-enabled device when you visit a website.

Cookies help websites function efficiently, improve user experience, remember user preferences, enhance website security, support embedded educational content, and provide information that helps improve website performance.

Cookies do not generally identify an individual directly. However, where cookies are associated with Personal Data, they are processed in accordance with the COMPUDON Junior Privacy Policy and the applicable provisions of the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023.

3

Types of Cookies We Use

Depending on how you use our website, we may use one or more of the following categories of cookies.

Essential Cookies

Essential cookies are necessary for the operation, security, and functionality of the website. These cookies may be used to:

  • Maintain secure user sessions
  • Authenticate users
  • Protect against fraudulent or unauthorised activity
  • Enable essential website functions

These cookies are necessary for the website to operate correctly and generally cannot be disabled without affecting website functionality.

Functional Cookies

Functional cookies remember user preferences and settings in order to improve the user experience. These cookies may remember:

  • Language preferences
  • Previously selected options
  • User interface settings
  • Other preferences that improve website usability

Analytics Cookies

Where enabled, analytics cookies help us understand how visitors use our website by collecting statistical or aggregated information. The information collected helps us:

  • Improve website performance
  • Identify technical issues
  • Enhance user experience
  • Improve educational services

Analytics information is used to improve our services and is not intended to identify individual users.

Third-Party Cookies and Similar Technologies

Certain features available on the COMPUDON Junior website may be provided through trusted third-party services. These services may use cookies or similar technologies in connection with:

  • Embedded educational videos
  • Social media integrations
  • Content delivery services
  • Communication tools
  • Other website functionality

CyberLearning Educational Society (Regd.) does not control the cookies or similar technologies used by independent third-party services. Users are encouraged to review the privacy policies of such providers before interacting with those services.

Certain embedded videos or social media features may not function correctly if cookies associated with those services are disabled.

4

How We Use Cookies

The cookies and similar technologies may be used for the following purposes:

  • Operating and securing the website
  • Maintaining user sessions
  • Remembering user preferences
  • Supporting embedded educational content
  • Improving website functionality
  • Measuring website performance
  • Understanding visitor interactions
  • Detecting technical problems
  • Supporting the delivery of educational services
We do not use cookies or similar technologies to:
  • Behaviourally profile children
  • Serve targeted advertising to children
  • Commercially exploit children's Personal Data
  • Process children's Personal Data in any manner prohibited under applicable law
5

Cookie Preferences

Where required by applicable law or where non-essential cookies are used, users may be provided with options to manage their cookie preferences through the website.

Users may also control cookies through the settings of their internet browser.

Please note that disabling certain cookies may affect the availability or functionality of some features of the website.

6

Third-Party Websites

The COMPUDON Junior website may contain links to third-party websites or provide access to third-party services. CyberLearning Educational Society (Regd.) is not responsible for the privacy practices, cookie practices, or content of independent third-party websites or services.

Users are encouraged to review the privacy policies and cookie policies of those organisations before providing Personal Data or interacting with their services.

7

Changes to this Cookie Policy

CyberLearning Educational Society (Regd.) may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in technology, website functionality, operational practices, legal requirements, or regulatory guidance.

The revised version will be published on the COMPUDON Junior website together with the updated "Last Updated" date. Where required by applicable law, appropriate notice of material changes will be provided.
