For Families

15 Questions Every Parent Should Ask About Their Child's Screen Habits

An evidence-based, age-appropriate checklist — prepared by Pankaj Rai, Founder & President, COMPUDON Junior. Based on published guidelines from AAP, WHO, APA, NCERT & NEP 2020.

Download printable PDF

Children at this stage are curious and trusting. They need guided exploration with clear guardrails.

❓ Layer 1: Developmental Readiness

Can your child do these things? If not, they may need more supervised access for now.

  • ☐Can explain why websites ask for their name or age
  • ☐Understands people online may not be who they say
  • ☐Comes to a trusted adult when uncomfortable online
  • ☐Understands anything shared online can stay permanently
  • ☐Can follow time-based screen rules without reminders
  • ☐Knows the difference between ads and real content
✅ Layer 2: Healthy Use Guidelines

Practical habits that build digital confidence without over-dependence.

  • ☐Screen time: max 1–2 hrs/day recreational (AAP)
  • ☐Devices in common family areas — not bedrooms at night
  • ☐Co-viewing: watch, play, explore together
  • ☐No personal social media (platforms require 13+)
  • ☐Parental controls active — explain why, like a seatbelt
  • ☐AI tools only with adult supervision at this age
⚠️ Layer 3: Red Flag Indicators

If you observe these consistently — start with curiosity, not confrontation.

  • ☐Unusually anxious or tearful when a device is taken away
  • ☐Hides the screen when an adult walks by
  • ☐Sleep disruption linked to screen use
  • ☐Loss of interest in activities they previously enjoyed
  • ☐Talks about online friends parents have never met
  • ☐Declining academic performance

Children at this stage seek autonomy and peer validation. They need more independence — with structure, conversations, and adult presence.

❓ Layer 1: Developmental Readiness

Before expanding digital independence, check these capabilities.

  • ☐Can critically evaluate online information — fact vs opinion
  • ☐Understands digital footprint and future impact
  • ☐Can resist peer pressure online
  • ☐Understands consent in digital contexts
  • ☐Can recognise clickbait, phishing, emotional triggers
  • ☐Understands free apps often profit from user data
✅ Layer 2: Healthy Use Guidelines

At this age, collaboration works better than control. Build the plan together.

  • ☐Create a family media plan together
  • ☐No devices in bedrooms after bedtime
  • ☐Search your child's name together online
  • ☐AI is a tool — verify answers, never submit as own work
  • ☐Review privacy settings and follower lists together
  • ☐Teach reporting and blocking — every platform has these
⚠️ Layer 3: Red Flag Indicators

Adolescence is turbulent — but these patterns warrant a calm conversation.

  • ☐Secretive — password changes, clearing history
  • ☐Dramatic mood shifts after phone use
  • ☐Chronic sleep deprivation from late-night use
  • ☐Multiple or hidden social media accounts
  • ☐Signs of cyberbullying — target or participant
  • ☐Mentions of self-harm, dares, or secret contacts

For teens especially: Your goal is to be the person they come to — not the person they hide from. Stay calm, avoid "I told you so," and focus on solutions. A single trusted adult can change the outcome.
