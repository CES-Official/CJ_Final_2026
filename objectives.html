Season VII Syllabus · Grades 3–8 (Ages 8–14)

Four pillars. Twelve modules. One future-ready child.

Explore what students master this season. Syllabus depth is tuned to age — Grades 3–5 and Grades 6–8 follow the same pillars at different levels.

Grades 3 to 5 · Explorers

Exam Objectives — Season VII

By Grade 3, children are already using YouTube, online games, messaging platforms, and AI-powered tools — long before they fully understand what is safe, accurate, or trustworthy. These objectives equip them with the skills they need.

Download Grades 3–5 PDF
DIGITAL SAFETY & ONLINE PROTECTION

I. Staying Safe During Online Interactions

  • Recognize and respond to online harassment
  • Recognize and avoid unsafe online behaviour
  • Recognize pressure tactics and tricks used by strangers online
  • Identify and report content that makes children uncomfortable or unsafe

II. Protecting Personal Information Online

  • Understand what a digital footprint is and how it is created
  • Manage and protect their digital footprint
  • Make careful decisions about what personal information to share online and with whom
CRITICAL THINKING & CREATIVITY

III. Thinking Critically and Asking Better Questions

  • Analyse evidence and facts to reach logical conclusions
  • Question assumptions before accepting information or taking action
  • Choose the right digital tool or format to share an idea clearly
  • Apply creative thinking when using technology to solve problems

IV. Growing as a Problem-Solver and Learner

  • Organise ideas clearly using digital tools
  • Try different approaches to solve problems and improve
  • Reflect on which strategies work best for their own learning
MEDIA LITERACY & ONLINE INFORMATION

V. Spotting False and Misleading Content

  • Identify when images, videos, and headlines online may be false or misleading
  • Recognise clickbait, exaggerated headlines, and content designed to trick or surprise
  • Practise simple techniques to check whether online content is likely to be true

VI. Thinking Before Believing or Sharing

  • Ask questions before accepting online content — who posted this, and why?
  • Recognise when online content is designed to influence how they feel or what they believe
  • Understand that apps and websites choose what to show based on what gets attention — not necessarily what is true or helpful
AI AWARENESS & RESPONSIBLE TECHNOLOGY USE

VII. Understanding AI and How It Works

  • Identify what AI is and explore the different kinds of AI tools encountered daily
  • Understand what AI can and cannot do
  • Recognise how AI uses information to give answers and why good information leads to better results
  • Explore how wrong or unclear information can lead to incorrect answers

VIII. Using AI Tools Safely and Wisely

  • Use simple AI tools safely and ask better questions
  • Check whether AI answers make sense before accepting them
  • Understand that AI answers may not always be correct
  • Use AI to support learning without copying or cheating
  • Respect others and behave responsibly while using technology

Grades 6 & Above · Navigators

Exam Objectives — Season VII

By Grade 6, students are managing social media, encountering AI-generated content daily, and navigating online spaces with real consequences. These objectives equip them with the skills they actually need.

Download Grades 6+ PDF
DIGITAL SAFETY & RESPONSIBLE ONLINE PARTICIPATION

I. Managing Unsafe and Harmful Online Interactions

  • Recognise and respond appropriately to online harassment, bullying, and unwanted contact
  • Understand risks including exploitation, grooming, and harmful content
  • Apply practical strategies to protect personal safety and wellbeing online
  • Know when and how to report unsafe situations

II. Evaluating Digital Content and Resisting Manipulation

  • Spot manipulative design in apps, games, platforms, and commercial content
  • Apply critical thinking to assess whether information is misleading, harmful, or age-inappropriate
  • Recognise how platforms use psychological techniques to keep users engaged and shape decisions

III. Protecting Privacy and Digital Identity

  • Understand what personal information is and why protecting it online matters
  • Manage a digital footprint responsibly and make informed choices about what to share and with whom
  • Recognise how online behaviour today can affect academic, professional, and personal opportunities
CRITICAL THINKING, CREATIVITY & LEARNING STRATEGIES

IV. Using Creativity to Support Learning

  • Apply creative thinking to explore concepts and solve problems
  • Try new approaches rather than relying on the same methods
  • Build confidence in thinking differently and producing original ideas

V. Designing Effective Learning Strategies

  • Organise ideas to understand and remember them better
  • Use pictures, diagrams, and creative formats to retain learning
  • Build personal strategies that work across subjects

VI. Growing Problem-Solving Skills

  • Break down complex problems using structured thinking
  • Try different strategies, reflect, and improve
  • Build self-awareness of how they learn best
MEDIA LITERACY & INFORMATION EVALUATION

VII. Spotting False or Misleading Information

  • Spot disinformation — exaggerated headlines, manipulated images, false statistics
  • Quickly check whether a source or content is credible
  • Recognise manipulated or deceptive content

VIII. Understanding How Online Information Can Influence Us

  • Recognise how emotions and biases affect decisions
  • Spot online tricks, clickbait, and misleading messages
  • Understand how biases influence decision-making
AI LITERACY & RESPONSIBLE TECHNOLOGY USE

IX. Understanding AI and Its Limitations

  • Understand what AI is and identify the different types of AI tools encountered in everyday life
  • Recognise what AI can and cannot do, including why it sometimes gets things wrong
  • Understand the risks of trusting AI outputs without checking them critically

X. How Information Helps AI

  • Learn how data is collected, organised, and used to train AI systems
  • Understand how the quality of information given to an AI directly affects the quality of its answers
  • Explore how wrong, unclear, or biased data can lead AI to produce incorrect or harmful outputs

XI. Using AI Tools Safely and Wisely

  • Use AI safely and responsibly
  • Ask better questions, evaluate responses critically
  • Know honest AI use versus cheating
  • Recognise privacy and safety risks

XII. Using AI in Safe and Responsible Ways

  • Understand ethical principles guiding AI development and use
  • Explore how AI affects fairness, privacy, and society
  • Behave responsibly and honestly while using technology
