About COMPUDON Junior

An international championship preparing children aged 8–14 for a safe, capable life online. Anchored in one principle: Think First.

Today's children are growing up in a world where every click, search, conversation, and AI interaction shapes their future. Success no longer depends only on knowing how to use technology - it depends on knowing how to use it wisely, safely, and responsibly.

COMPUDON Junior was created to help children build those essential life skills.

Designed for students aged 8 to 14, COMPUDON Junior is a Digital Citizenship Championship that challenges learners to think critically, stay safe online, use artificial intelligence responsibly, protect their privacy, evaluate digital information, and develop the technology skills needed to succeed in an increasingly connected world.

Inspired by Microsoft's Digital Literacy Standard IV, the program has evolved beyond teaching foundational ICT skills. Today, its curriculum integrates digital literacy, cyber safety, media literacy, AI literacy, critical thinking, privacy awareness, and responsible digital citizenship into an engaging, age-appropriate learning experience.

The championship is conducted across three progressive levels, allowing students to demonstrate their knowledge and practical understanding while building confidence through practice assessments designed around the championship format. More than a competition, it is a journey of learning that prepares young people for lifelong success in education, work, and society.

Beginning with Season VII, COMPUDON Junior expands onto the international stage. The top three eligible winners from each participating country will earn the opportunity to represent their school and nation at the International Finals, competing for more than 100 prestigious awards while celebrating excellence in digital citizenship.

Built on Globally Recognized Education Frameworks

Microsoft Digital Literacy Standard IV NEP 2020 NCF 2023 CBSE Competency-Based Education UNICEF Digital Learning Framework OECD Learning Compass 2030 UNESCO Digital Citizenship ISTE Student Standards

Originally inspired by Microsoft's Digital Literacy Standard IV, the COMPUDON Junior curriculum has evolved into a comprehensive digital citizenship framework. Its learning outcomes are systematically mapped to the National Education Policy (NEP) 2020, the National Curriculum Framework (NCF) 2023, relevant CBSE circulars and competency-based learning expectations, the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023, and internationally recognized digital literacy and media literacy frameworks. This ensures that students develop age-appropriate competencies in digital literacy, cyber safety, AI literacy, media literacy, critical thinking, privacy awareness, and responsible digital citizenship.

COMPUDON Junior is an initiative of CyberLearning Educational Society (CES), the organization behind COMPUDON - India's first official Microsoft Office Skills Championship, which introduced thousands of students to internationally recognized standards in Microsoft Word, Excel, and PowerPoint through live, hands-on, skill-based assessments.

About CyberLearning Educational Society (CES)

CyberLearning Educational Society (CES), established on July 8, 2005, is a non-profit organization dedicated to advancing digital literacy, cyber safety, critical thinking, and digital citizenship among children and young people. Our mission is to empower every learner with the knowledge, skills, and confidence needed to thrive safely and responsibly in an increasingly digital world.

CES works closely with India CyberLearning Pvt. Ltd., a Microsoft Global Training Partner and Microsoft Training Services Partner, to leverage industry expertise, globally recognized learning pathways, and technology-enabled education initiatives that benefit students, schools, and communities.

For more than two decades, CES has collaborated with organizations including Microsoft, Intel, Huawei, the Times Foundation, 100+ schools, and more than 50 Rotary Clubs across Rotary International Districts 3010, 3011, and 3012 to expand access to digital skills education for students from underserved and economically disadvantaged communities.

Through these and other partnerships, CES has supported digital literacy initiatives and facilitated globally recognized certification opportunities, helping thousands of young learners build future-ready skills. Collectively, these initiatives have enabled training and certification scholarships valued at more than ₹2 crore.

Today, CES collaborates with schools, educators, government agencies, CSR partners, and community organizations to deliver impactful programs that promote digital literacy, cyber safety, responsible AI, critical thinking, online privacy, and digital citizenship, preparing children and young people to participate confidently, safely, and responsibly in the digital economy.

Think First. — the philosophy underneath everything

Before you click. Before you share. Before you trust. Think First. These two words anchor every module, every lesson, and every habit COMPUDON Junior builds. Not fear — judgement. Not restriction — capability. The skill of pausing before reacting is the foundation everything else rests on.
