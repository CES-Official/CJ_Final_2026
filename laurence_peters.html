About Mr. Drew Williams

Dr. Laurence C. Peters brings a multidisciplinary background to the field of education policy, with experience spanning law, legislative service, and federal education administration. He is a graduate of the University of Michigan and the University of Maryland School of Law. He previously served as Counsel to the U.S. House of Representatives Subcommittee on Select Education and Civil Rights.

He later spent nearly two decades at the U.S. Department of Education as a senior policy advisor, where his work focused on systemic equity and the capacity of public institutions to serve historically underserved student populations.

Dr. Peters is currently Adjunct Associate Professor at the Johns Hopkins School of Education and Vice President of Next Generation Inc., a Maryland-based workforce development organization dedicated to expanding access to careers in the clean energy sector for emerging and transitioning workers.

His scholarship engages questions of international education policy and global governance. He is the author of a volume on the United Nations published by Palgrave Macmillan and co-author, with Himanshi Sharma of India, of the forthcoming book Future Schools Now! (Johns Hopkins University Press), reflecting a commitment to cross-national collaboration in education reform.

Dr. Peters serves on the COMPUDON Junior Academic and Policy Advisory Board in alignment with its focus on advancing rigorous, socially grounded learning and fostering the next generation of globally engaged scholars and practitioners.
