Mr. Drew Williams

Cybersecurity & Defense Expert · Advisory Board

Drew Williams is a U.S. Navy veteran and cybersecurity pioneer whose career began as a Defense Information Specialist monitoring Soviet naval activity in the Indian Ocean from 1979 to 1982. Following his military service, he launched one of the IT security industry's first Host Intrusion Detection Systems (HIDS), one of the earliest leading SIEM platforms, and co-founded the Symantec SWAT team — among the first dedicated Security Services and Hacker Research units in the world.

With decades of frontline experience defending against evolving digital threats, Mr. Williams brings rare, real-world credibility to COMPUDON Junior's mission of equipping young people with cyber safety, media literacy and critical-thinking skills.

He helped establish the OpSec security alliance with Check Point Software Technologies, built the Center for Policy and Compliance for VMware, and designed post-graduate Cyber Security degree programmes for universities across the US and Southeast Asia.

His understanding of how cyber threats develop and spread directly informs the organisation's approach to building age-appropriate, meaningful digital-literacy programmes — and his discipline and extensive industry network expand the programme's credibility and reach. Mr. Williams is committed to ensuring every young person has the knowledge and tools to navigate the digital world safely and confidently.
