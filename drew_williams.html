About Mr. Drew Williams

Drew Williams is a U.S. Navy Veteran and cybersecurity pioneer whose career began as a Defense Information Specialist monitoring Soviet naval activity in the Indian Ocean from 1979 to 1982. Following his military service, he went on to launch one of the IT security industry's first Host Intrusion Detection Systems (HIDS), one of the earliest leading SIEM platforms, and co-founded the Symantec SWAT team, among the first dedicated Security Services and Hacker Research units in the world.

With decades of frontline experience defending against evolving digital threats, Mr. Williams brings rare, real-world credibility to Compudon Junior's mission of equipping young people with cyber safety, media literacy, and critical thinking skills.

He helped establish the OpSec security alliance with Check Point Software Technologies, established the Center for Policy and Compliance for VMWare and designed post-graduate degree programs in Cyber Security for several Universities in the US and Southeast Asia.

His deep understanding of how cyber threats develop and spread directly informs the organization's approach to building age-appropriate, meaningful digital literacy programs that prepare students for the challenges of today's connected world

His military background reinforces the discipline and critical thinking at the heart of Compudon Junior's educational philosophy, while his extensive industry network opens doors to partnerships and opportunities that expand the program's credibility and reach.

Mr. Williams is committed to ensuring that every young person has the knowledge and tools to navigate the digital world safely and confidently.
